The Problem

The road is modern. The truth is not.

Every day, people set out believing they will return safely. Yet when collisions happen, truth disappears in seconds. Memories blur, stories diverge, and trust breaks. Families grieve; companies argue; cities pay the price. What’s missing is not more noise — it’s a common, dependable account of what truly occurred.

This absence of clarity turns accidents into conflicts. Claims slow down. Fraud grows. Honest drivers carry suspicion while real victims are left defending themselves. In a century ruled by data, the place that connects us all — the road — still runs in shadows.

We don’t need more surveillance. We need integrity: evidence designed to be fair, private, and accepted by everyone who depends on it.
